About this project

This Project was part of a 24 week UI/UX course through the University of Oregon.

Role: As part of a team of three designers, our roles were largely interchangeable throughout the User Research, User Interface Design, and Usability Testing phases.

Project Duration: 4 weeks

The Problem: The outdated design of the Hollywood Farmer’s Market website no longer reflects their community accurately due to a shifting demographic. Infrequent updates to market information are also putting a strain on vender/shopper relationships while also leading to an underutilization of market food benefits.

How has the demographic shifted? What kind of redesign will help the market keep up to pace with the growth of the community?

The Design Process

Research

Methodology

Our methodology will involve an interview process that synthesizes the goals of the stakeholders and users alike.

A google survey will also be deployed to reach the food benefit recipient demographic more anonymously, with the purpose of uncovering any potential hurdles that result in underutilization of these benefits at this farmer’s market.

User Interviews

Our team conducted 5 user interviews, with the purpose of redesigning and supporting the need for a site that is easy to maintain for a local farmers market, in collaboration with stakeholders and users.

Goals

Support the mission of the Hollywood Farmers Market by improving access to HFM events and announcements for the community.

Core Questions

  1. What information do users want to see on the HFM website?
  2. What information do stakeholders want to see on the HFM website?
  3. What content should be regularly updated?
  4. What information is currently hard to access on the website?
  5. What information is currently accessible on the website?

Our Findings

Current imagery is confusing.

Certain information seemed vague or outdated.

Alignment issues are visible througout the homepage.

Key information such as time and location were at least easy to find.

Stakeholder Interview

Our team conducted an interview with a key stakeholder of the organization to uncover their current pain points and fact-find on market data.

Areas of interest to discuss included:

Immediate Goals

  • What information do you think is most important for your users to get from your website?
  • What would be the most important, or two most important, call to actions on the site?

Current State

  • What are some areas of growth or improvement from your current website?
  • Is there anything on your current website that you would like to remove completely?

Looking Ahead

  • What is on your wishlist for a new website?
  • Do you have brand style guidelines that we could integrate?
  • Are there any websites that you’ve seen that you’d like to emulate?

Stakeholder Needs

Reflect a shifting demographic in the neighborhood and in shoppers.

Invoke feelings of joy, fun, & freshness; add color for liveliness.

Ability to update website quickly and frequently.

Include sponsor logos to promote participation in sponsorship.

Survey

While conducting our interviews, we deployed an anonymous survey to give us additional clues about the demographic we were dealing with, as well as pain points and motivators for their farmers market experiences, both locally and online. Our survey recorded data from 18 participants in the Portland metro area.

Survey Findings

  • The majority of participants were between the age of 30 – 39 years old.
  • The participants were primarily Hollywood farmers market goers.
  • The market's website trailed significantly behind social media as a primary source for market updates.
  • While most participants new about the market's acceptance of SNAP benefits, a majority of them couldn't weigh in on its utilization.
