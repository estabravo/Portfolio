About this project

This Project was part of a 24 week UI/UX course through the University of Oregon.

Role: As part of a team of three designers, our roles were largely interchangeable throughout the User Research, User Interface Design, and Usability Testing phases.

Project Duration: 4 weeks

The Problem: The outdated design of the Hollywood Farmer’s Market website no longer reflects their community accurately due to a shifting demographic. Infrequent updates to market information are also putting a strain on vender/shopper relationships while also leading to an underutilization of market food benefits.

How has the demographic shifted? What kind of redesign will help the market keep up to pace with the growth of the community?

The Design Process

Research

Methodology

Our methodology will involve an interview process that synthesizes the goals of the stakeholders and users alike.

A google survey will also be deployed to reach the food benefit recipient demographic more anonymously, with the purpose of uncovering any potential hurdles that result in underutilization of these benefits at this farmer’s market.

User Interviews

Our team conducted 5 user interviews, with the purpose of redesigning and supporting the need for a site that is easy to maintain for a local farmers market, in collaboration with stakeholders and users.

Goals

Support the mission of the Hollywood Farmers Market by improving access to HFM events and announcements for the community.

Core Questions

  1. What information do users want to see on the HFM website?
  2. What information do stakeholders want to see on the HFM website?
  3. What content should be regularly updated?
  4. What information is currently hard to access on the website?
  5. What information is currently accessible on the website?

Our Findings

Current imagery is confusing.

Certain information seemed vague or outdated.

Alignment issues are visible througout the homepage.

Key information such as time and location were at least easy to find.

Stakeholder Interview

Our team conducted an interview with a key stakeholder of the organization to uncover their current pain points and fact-find on market data.

Areas of interest to discuss included:

Immediate Goals

  • What information do you think is most important for your users to get from your website?
  • What would be the most important, or two most important, call to actions on the site?

Current State

  • What are some areas of growth or improvement from your current website?
  • Is there anything on your current website that you would like to remove completely?

Looking Ahead

  • What is on your wishlist for a new website?
  • Do you have brand style guidelines that we could integrate?
  • Are there any websites that you’ve seen that you’d like to emulate?

Stakeholder Needs

Reflect a shifting demographic in the neighborhood and in shoppers.

Invoke feelings of joy, fun, & freshness; add color for liveliness.

Ability to update website quickly and frequently.

Include sponsor logos to promote participation in sponsorship.

Survey

While conducting our interviews, we deployed an anonymous survey to give us additional clues about the demographic we were dealing with, as well as pain points and motivators for their farmers market experiences, both locally and online. Our survey recorded data from 18 participants in the Portland metro area.

Survey Findings

  • The majority of participants were between the age of 30 – 39 years old.
  • The participants were primarily Hollywood farmers market goers.
  • The market's website trailed significantly behind social media as a primary source for market updates.
  • While most participants new about the market's acceptance of SNAP benefits, a majority of them couldn't weigh in on its utilization.

Definition & Ideation

User Insights

Pain Points

Problem Definition

Portland's Hollywood Farmers Market's current website does not align with their brand standards and has out-of-date information. It needs to more effectively showcase the welcoming culture and diverse community of the market.

Solutions

Give users a preview of the market experience.

Keep information current for reliability.

Stakeholder needs to be able to update the website.

How might we embody the market experience on the website and promote a greater sense of community?

Neighborhood Market Analysis

Because Portland Farmers Market's are all part of the same coalition, we felt it necessary to tweak the SWOT approach of analyzing to be less threatened by our neighbors in the Portland Metro. Instead, an inventory of specific features, along with a heuristic oriented redlining gave us plenty to analyze.

Ideation

Prioritizing Our Features

With more well-formed insights in hand, we were able to begin our brainstorming with "I like, I wish, What if?" to get the creative juices going.

Being that the organization had a long-term goal of changing their website host from Wordpress to Squarespace, we also had to be mindful of the platform's technologically constraints, amidst their rigid design parameters.

Thankfully, there were several advantage of the platform that aligned strongly with our "Wow!" quadrant, such as updatability.

User Flows and Site Map

We began to carve out the user experience as a team, by iterating a user flow that would not only address our user and stakeholder's needs, but do so intuitively.

To map out our site, we also took a hierarchal approach. Taking the guess work out of locating the most important information for our users was our top priority, which is why ensuring nothing outdated or irrelevant would negatively impact the user experience.

Design

Sketches

The time we spent in search of inspiration, scouring competitor trends, and mood-boarding on InVision, fueled our creativity to begin iterating user focused designs.

We wanted something modern, that would meet such needs as having important information front and center, giving users a preview of the market with thoughtfully placed image sections, while still drawing attention to their newsletter and donating with CTA buttons.

We took each other's strongest ideas to emerge with a consensus for our homepage's layout.

Testing & Iterating

Upon styling our page, and integrating interactions, we began testing our design with users to gauge our efforts in improving the user experience thus far.

We iterated on our design based off of the feedback collected from our user tests, and then presented it to our stakeholder for review and final thoughts. Below are some of our key findings, and iterations based on our process.

Looking Ahead

Impactful Takeaways

The Hollywood Farmers Market website redesign gave us an opportunity to work not only with users, but a stakeholder as well, granting us invaluable insight on the scope of the end product.

  • Our biggest fear was also our greatest reward: earning the trust of the stakeholder for firsthand experience and further collaboration.
  • The feedback and excitement we received over the results was the greatest reward.

Further Development

We plan to take things one step further by iterating on our finished design, and running through our process for additional pages for the website.

As the business plan was to apply these changes during the farmers market's move from hosting their site on Wordpress to Squarespace, we hope to bring our redesign to life by pitching our finished product to the market to negotiate the acquisition of our design.
